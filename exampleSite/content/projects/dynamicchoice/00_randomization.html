Abstract
Summary of randomization code and results for students not enrolled in Higher Education.

Population

For this study, we will send information to all applicants that have valid scores (either took the Transition Test PDT 2022 or the Access to Higher Education Test PAES 2022) but that are not currently enrolled in any higher education institution (until XX May 2023). We include the following groups for un-enrolled students:

  1. Applicants with preferences (either from the survey or through their applications):
    • S1.1 Complete survey: Applicants who completed the survey and selected they were interested in taking the PAES 2023 (N = 2854).
    • S1.2 In-progress survey: Applicants who started (but not completed) the survey and either selected that they were interested in taking the PAES 2023 or left that question blank (N = 2591).
    • S1.3 Non-respondent: Applicants who didn’t respond to the survey, but have preferences through their 2023 application (N = 56153).
  2. Applicants without preferences (neither from the survey or through their applications):
    • S2.1 In-progress survey: Applicants who started (but not completed) the survey and either selected that they were interested in taking the PAES 2023 or left that question blank, but didn’t respond to preferences on the survey or applied to programs in 2023 (N = 2184).
    • S2.2 Non-respondent: Applicants who didn’t respond to the survey and didn’t apply to any programs in 2023 (N = 69418).

From the original population described above, we only exclude respondents that explicitly declared they were not interested in taking the PAES 2023. We will also exclude applicants that declared not wanting to participate in studies when they enrolled in the PDT 2022 or PAES 20221.

Randomization

Considering the previous groups, we will randomize each group in a 40%-60% split for treatment and control. We consider a slightly higher share of control students because the original control group will be divided in a later stage into pure control and static information.

In this case, only applicants with recorded preferences can be randomized into the full treatment (personalized information), but we will also randomize applicants without recorded preferences into getting a portion of the treatment.

Randomization procedure

  • We sort applicants by their year of high school graduation, their average maximum score in math and language (deciles), and the difference between their actual score and the cutoff score for their first preference (20 groups by percentiles).

  • We create groups of 5 applicants according to their sorting order (group 1: 1-5, group 2: 6-10, etc.), and within those groups we randomize a variable from 1 to 5 (without replacement).

    • In case that there is a group smaller than 5, the variable is still randomized from 1 to 5 (and some random values are not used).

  • If the random order assignment is 1 or 2, the applicant is assigned to treatment. If the random order assignment is 3, 4, or 5, the applicant is assigned to the control group.

Applicants with recorded preferences

In this case, we have three groups with recorded preferences: (i) Applicants that completed the survey (S1.1), (ii) applicants that started the survey but did not finish (S1.2), and (iii) applicants that did not start the survey (S1.3)
